Why pay more for same MP?
Same resolution (5MP) across all 3 cards destroys the reason cards exist. A customer looks at all 3, sees "5MP" everywhere, and asks "what am I actually paying extra for?" — then calls you instead of clicking. Cards exist to let customers self-select without calling.

Current wizard questions — audit

1
Property type (Home / Shop / Office / Factory)
✓ Good — this drives the right recommendation. Keep it. Add "Society / Housing complex" as an option, it's a big segment in Jaipur.
2
Number of cameras
✓ Good — but add a helper: "Not sure? Tell us your floor count + entry points and we'll suggest." Many customers don't know how many they need.
3
Indoor / Outdoor / Both
✓ Good — this affects camera type (IP66/IP67 rating). Keep it.
4
IP camera / HD camera (technology choice)
⚠ Problem: Customer doesn't know the difference. Asking this question early causes confusion and drop-off. Move this question AFTER showing them the 3-card comparison. Let them see the price and benefit difference first, then choose. The wizard should educate, not quiz.
5
MISSING — Budget range
Critical missing question for Indian market. Show 4 options: "Under ₹30K / ₹30K–₹75K / ₹75K–₹1.5L / No limit". This single question lets you pre-filter which card to highlight. Without it, you're recommending 4K to someone with ₹25K budget.
6
MISSING — New installation or upgrade?
If it's an upgrade of an existing DVR system, recommending IP/NVR means new wiring cost (₹8K–₹20K extra). Customer needs to know this upfront. HD cameras reuse existing coaxial cable — huge saving. This question changes the entire recommendation.
7
MISSING — Special requirement (single most important question)
Options: "Just basic monitoring / See faces clearly / Read vehicle number plates / Cover large area (warehouse/parking) / All of the above". This single answer determines minimum MP requirement. A jeweller who says "read number plates" needs 8MP at the entrance — you can't give them 2MP and then blame the camera later.
8
MISSING — Remote viewing (internet available?)
IP cameras require a stable internet connection for remote viewing on phone. If the customer's site has poor internet (common in industrial areas, storage facilities), IP-over-WiFi will fail. Ask: "Do you have broadband internet at the installation site?" If no → recommend HD system or local-access-only IP setup.
9
Night vision / low-light coverage
⚠ If this question exists, it's not weighted correctly in recommendations. Infrared vs full-colour night vision is a ₹3,000–₹8,000 per camera difference. Customer should understand: "Basic IR (black/white at night)" vs "Color Night Vision (Hikvision AcuSense/Dahua WizSense)" — show them a real comparison image.

→ Based on Q4+Q5+Q6, auto-select and highlight the right card. Show HD comparison note if Q4=upgrade OR Q6=under ₹30K.

Should you show HD quotation when customer selects IP only?

Yes, always show HD as a "save money" comparison — but frame it correctly: "Here's what you selected (IP 4MP). Here's the HD equivalent at 20–30% lower cost. For your use case, we recommend IP because..." This is honest, builds trust, and prevents buyer's remorse after installation.

Quote page — what customers struggle to understand

1
Technical model numbers confuse customers
Customers don't know what "DS-2CD2143G2-I" means. Replace with: "Hikvision 4MP Outdoor Dome Camera" with a small photo. Model number goes in a "technical details" expandable section — hidden by default. 80% of customers don't need it.
2
Storage shown as "2TB HDD" — meaningless to customer
Translate: "2TB storage = 45 days of recording with 6 cameras (H.265)". Every customer wants to know "how many days back can I watch?" — give them that answer directly on the quote. This is a conversion booster — it shows they're getting real value.
3
No visual of what 4MP vs 2MP actually looks like
Add a simple before/after comparison image on the quote page showing a face at 5 metres in 2MP vs 4MP. One image = 1000 words. Customer immediately understands why they're paying more. This is the single highest-ROI UX addition you can make to the quote page.
4
Price breakdown doesn't show "what I'm saving vs market rate"
Add a single line: "Market rate for this setup: ₹95,000 – ₹1,10,000. Your TEAM CCTV price: ₹74,000." This anchors the customer to a higher reference price and makes your quote feel like a deal. Common practice in US/UK CCTV companies — not done in India yet.
5
Validity period is shown — good
✓ The "Valid for 7 days" with countdown is excellent urgency-building UX. Keep it. Make the countdown pulse red when ≤3 days remain.
6
No WhatsApp "Accept this quote" option
In India, many customers (especially 45+) will not click a button on a website to "accept". They want to call or WhatsApp. Add a WhatsApp button: "Message us on WhatsApp to confirm" next to the Accept button. This alone will increase conversions from quote views significantly for the Jaipur market.
7
Quote page doesn't show the assigned sales person
Show: "Your TEAM contact: Rahul Sharma · +91 98290 XXXXX". A real person's name and photo builds 3× more trust than a company number. The customer has been through a wizard, they feel they have a relationship — confirm it by showing who will call them.
